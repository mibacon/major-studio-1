Defining NYC Trash

BY Michal Bacon
How should New Yorkers be recycling their trash?
What do they really do?

Each year, New Yorkers generate over 3 million tons of trash. In fact, NYC generates more trash than any other city in the world. Mayor DeBlasio has pledged that by 2030, New York will send ZERO Waste to landfills. To see if and how this is possible, we must first take a closer look at what's in our trash bins.

What are the main streams of trash?

NYC trash is divided into three main categories, recognized by the color of the garbage bin: brown/black for refuse sent to landfills; green for metal, glass and plastic sent for recycling; and blue for paper, sent for recycling as well. Click below to learn what is in your trash.

Missed Opportunities

Over 30% of all trash that ends up in landfills should have been recycled. If New Yorkers learned to recycle properly, over 600,000 tons of trash a year would be diverted from landfills. However, even if New Yorkers were to recycle with 100% accuracy, there would still be 2.5 million tons of trash sent to landfills each year. However, by begining new programs to divert other types of refuse, we will chip away at this number.

Recycling is not the future

Nearly 32% of all trash sent to landfills is Organics Suitable for Composting. If New Yorkers would begin composting, nearly 1 million tons of trash would be diverted from landfills. A pilot program has already begun in NYC but is limited. To reach Zero Waste, such pilot programs will need to be implemented citywide.
